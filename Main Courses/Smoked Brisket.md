# Smoked Brisket

Source: Serious Eats

## Ingredients

- 10-12 lb. whole brisket, trimmed
- 1/4 cup kosher salt
- 2 tbsp black pepper
- charcoal and wood chunks for smoking
- disposable aluminum pan
- 6 cups water
- probe thermometer
- 1 cup beef stock
- parchment or butcher paper
- heavy duty aluminum foil

## Prep

- Mix salt & pepper and coat brisket with rub
- Refrigerate on wire rack for 12-24 hours
- Add charcoal to grill using snake method
  - Make a larger snake for winter smoking
- Place pan in middle and pour water into it
- Clean & oil the grill grate

## Directions

- Start some charcoals in chimney and start the snake
- Position thermometer in middle of brisket
- Add brisket to grate over pan, fat side up
- Cover and adjust until temp is 225-275 F
- Smoke until brisket reaches 160-170 F
  - 6-10 hours
  - Spritz with beef stock every hour
- Remove brisket and wrap with single parchment and double foil
- Add more water to pan, if needed
- Return to grill, fat side up, and continue until 195-200 F
  - 2-4 hours
  - Alternately, move to 225 F oven to finish cooking
- Remove and let rest, covered, for 2-3 hours
  - Can rest inside cooler to keep brisket warm
- Transfer to carving board and slice against the grain
